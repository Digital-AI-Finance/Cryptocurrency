\activitybox{Activity 1: Explore NFT Marketplaces}{10 min}{%
Visit \url{https://opensea.io} and explore the marketplace. Answer:
\begin{enumerate}
\item What are the top 3 collections by total volume? What is the floor price of each?
\item Pick one collection --- how many total items does it have? How many unique owners?
\item Click on any individual NFT --- what metadata does it show (traits, rarity, history)?
\item Find the ``Activity'' tab --- what does ``wash trading'' look like in transaction history?
\end{enumerate}
\textbf{Bonus:} Compare OpenSea with \url{https://blur.io}. How do their fee structures differ?
}

\activitybox{Activity 2: NFT Metadata Investigation}{5 min}{%
Look up a CryptoPunk or Bored Ape Yacht Club NFT on \url{https://etherscan.io}. Answer:
\begin{enumerate}
\item Find the smart contract address. What token standard does it implement (ERC-721 or ERC-1155)?
\item Look at the tokenURI function output. Where does the metadata point to (IPFS, HTTP, on-chain)?
\item If the metadata points to IPFS, what happens if the IPFS gateway goes down?
\item What is the difference between on-chain and off-chain metadata storage?
\end{enumerate}

% ── Glossary ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
\section{Key Terms}

\renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.25}
\noindent\begin{tabular}{@{}p{3.2cm}p{11.4cm}@{}}
\toprule
\textbf{Term} & \textbf{Definition} \\
\midrule
\textbf{NFT}                  & Non-Fungible Token. A cryptographic token on a blockchain that represents a unique asset; unlike fungible tokens, each NFT is distinct and not interchangeable. \\
\textbf{ERC-721}              & The Ethereum token standard for non-fungible tokens. Each token has a unique ID and owner, making it suitable for digital collectibles and art. \\
\textbf{ERC-1155}             & A multi-token standard allowing a single contract to manage both fungible and non-fungible tokens, reducing gas costs for batch transfers. \\
\textbf{Metadata}             & The descriptive data associated with an NFT (name, image, traits, description). Can be stored on-chain, on IPFS, or on centralised servers. \\
\textbf{Floor Price}          & The lowest current asking price for any NFT within a collection, used as a benchmark for minimum market value. \\
\textbf{Minting}              & The process of creating an NFT by recording it on the blockchain. Minting assigns a unique token ID and transfers ownership to the creator or buyer. \\
\textbf{Royalties}            & A percentage of secondary sale proceeds automatically paid to the original creator via smart contract logic, enabling ongoing revenue. \\
\textbf{IPFS}                 & InterPlanetary File System. A distributed peer-to-peer protocol for storing and sharing content-addressed files, commonly used to host NFT metadata and images. \\
\textbf{Wash Trading}         & A form of market manipulation where a single party (or coordinated parties) repeatedly buys and sells the same asset to artificially inflate volume or price. \\
\textbf{Soulbound Token (SBT)} & A non-transferable NFT permanently bound to one wallet, proposed for representing credentials, memberships, or identity attributes. \\
\textbf{Rarity Score}         & A numerical measure of how uncommon an NFT's combination of traits is within its collection; higher scores indicate rarer attributes. \\
\textbf{Token URI}            & A function in ERC-721/ERC-1155 contracts that returns the URL or on-chain data pointing to an NFT's metadata JSON file. \\
\bottomrule
\end{tabular}
